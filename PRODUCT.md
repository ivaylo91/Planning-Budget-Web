# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Bulgarian-speaking everyday shoppers (the app is fully localized in Bulgarian, prices shown in EUR) who want to keep their grocery spending under control. They open the app frequently — often daily or several times a week — to check their budget status, browse current promotions across 7 retail chains (Billa, Lidl, Kaufland, Fantastiko, T Market, Metro, CBA), build a shopping list before heading to the store, and review how much they spent and saved afterward. It's a mobile-first PWA used in short, frequent sessions (checking a number, adding an item, ticking off a purchase), not long browsing sessions.

## Product Purpose

"Пазарувай умно" ("Shop smart") helps people set a grocery budget, discover real active promotions, plan a shopping trip around them, and track how much of their budget they've spent and saved over time — turning scattered weekly-flyer browsing into one clear, ongoing picture of their grocery spend.

## Brand Personality

Polished consumer-app feel: premium but everyday, in the spirit of modern banking/fintech apps (Revolut/N26-style) — clean, confident, modern without being cold. It should feel trustworthy with the user's money (precise numbers, calm color use, no gimmicks) while still feeling encouraging about the small wins (hitting a discount, staying under budget, growing savings over time).

## Anti-references

Avoid the feel of generic spreadsheet-style budgeting tools (dense, gray, joyless) and of templated SaaS dashboards (hero-metric clichés, flat identical cards, gradient accents). The app should feel considered and specific to grocery shopping in Bulgaria, not like a reskinned generic finance template.

## Design Principles

- **Respect the money**: numbers are the product — budget, spend, savings, prices must read with clear hierarchy and immediate trust. Precision over decoration.
- **Celebrate small wins**: hitting a promo, staying under budget, growing savings should feel acknowledged (subtle, not loud) — this is what makes a recurring habit feel rewarding rather than like bookkeeping.
- **Built for frequent, short visits**: this is opened often for quick checks and quick actions (add item, mark purchased, glance at remaining budget). Every interaction should feel immediate — fast feedback, no friction, nothing that gets old on the hundredth use.
- **Bulgarian-first, not translated**: copy, currency (EUR), categories and store names are native to the Bulgarian grocery market — the design should feel made for this market, not adapted from an English-first template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Solid defaults: WCAG-level contrast (≥4.5:1 body text), full keyboard navigation, and `prefers-reduced-motion` support throughout. No specific user accommodations beyond that have been flagged.
